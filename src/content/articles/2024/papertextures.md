**This tutorial will show you how to create an authentic crumpled paper texture animation in Adobe After Effects CC 2025 for a stop-motion or hand-drawn look.

Trim Layers: Opt/Alt + \] (square bracket)  
Set End Work Area: N  
Composition Settings: Cmd/Ctrl + K

**Expression Used:**

**How To Crumple Paper & Scan:**

1.  Take 3-10 pieces of paper you want to animate as background
2.  Crumple each piece of paper by hand and then unfold to prepare to digitize
3.  Use a Scanner set to 300 DPI + Black & White or, take a photo and send to computer

**How To Import Crumpled Paper Texture:**

1.  Open After Effects
2.  Create New Composition (Cmd/Ctrl + N)
3.  Composition Settings: 1920x1080, 24fps, 5 seconds
4.  Double-click on empty space in Project Panel to open Finder/Explorer
5.  Select Paper texture files scanned to computer
6.  Click ‘Open’
7.  Drag All Paper Textures on Composition (or use Cmd/Ctrl + /)

**How To Animate Crumpled Paper Texture:**

1.  Select All Layers
2.  Rotate & Scale to fit Composition if necessary
3.  Tap Cmd/Ctrl + Right Arrow 4 times to move CTI 4 frames
4.  Press Shift + Cmd/Ctrl + Right Square Bracket to Trim Layers
5.  Go to bottom layer and select
6.  Press Shift Key and select top layer
7.  Right-click and select ‘Keyframe Assistant’
8.  Select ’Sequence Layers’
9.  Press OK
10. Drag CTI to the last layer
11. Press N to Set Work Area
12. Tap Spacebar to Preview

**How To Loop:**

1.  Select all Layers
2.  Right-click and select ‘PreCompose’ (Move All Layers)
3.  Right-click on PreComp and select ‘Time’
4.  Select ‘Enable Time Remapping’
5.  Go to last keyframe and tap Cmd/Ctrl + left arrow to move back 1 frame
6.  Clic the diamond to add a keyframe
7.  Select Last keyframe and delete it
8.  Press Option/Alt and click on the stopwatch next to Time Remapping
9.  Enter loopOut() to endlessly loop animation
10. Press Cmd + K to open Comp Settings
11. Enter 60, to increase duration of Comp to 1 minute
12. Drag out PreComp to fill out Timeline

**Render Animation:**

1.  Go to File…Export…Add To Render Queue
2.  Go to ‘Render Settings’
3.  Click on ‘Best Settings’
4.  Go to ‘Time Sampling’ section
5.  Change ‘Format’ to H.264 40 Mbps (or preferred Preset)
6.  Click on ‘Output To:’ and add title and set destination to Render
7.  Click on ‘Render’
8.  Preview Animation
