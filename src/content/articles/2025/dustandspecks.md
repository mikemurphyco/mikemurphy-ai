**How To Extract Specks in Adobe Photoshop CC 2025:**

1.  Scan in ChipBoard or Paper with good fiber/grain
2.  600 DPI…Black & White…Jpeg
3.  Add Levels Adjustment Layer
4.  Drag Black & White Sliders so background is white and specks black
5.  Add Gausian Blur (2-5 px)
6.  Image…Mode…Greyscale
7.  Image…Mode…Bitmap
8.  600 PPI, 50% Threshold…Click OK
9.  Select Layer
10. Go to Select Menu at the top and Click ‘Color Range’
11. Choose ‘Shadows’ and click OK to select all black specks
12. Click on 1/2 moon icon and select ‘Solid Color’ and change to Black
13. Rename Layer ‘Black Specks’
14. Duplicate Layer and change color to White
15. Rename Layer ‘White Specks’
16. Go to Image in top menu…Image Size…Change to 300 DPI
17. Add Speck Layers to Adobe CC Libraries
18. Convert Layers to Smart Objects
19. Export each as a PNG with Transparency

**How To Animate Specks in After Effects**

1.  Create New Comp
2.  1920 x 1080, 24 fps, 10 secs
3.  Import Speck PNG files or drag from Adobe CC Library on Comps
4.  Select White Speck Layer
5.  Press P for Position Property
6.  Press Shift + R for Rotation Property
7.  Press Option/Alt and click on Stopwatch next to Position
8.  Enter **  
    **posterizeTime(8); wiggle(5,300);
9.  Copy Expression & Paste into Rotation Property
10. Add Expression on Position & Rotation of Black Speck Layer
11. Tap Spacebar to Preview

**How To Create ‘Old Film Hairs’ in After Effects:**

1.  Duplicate Black Speck Layer
2.  Add Gausian Blur Effect (2 px)
3.  Add Turbulent Displace (increase amount to ~ 150)
4.  Increase blur or reduce opacity to taste

**How To Export Animated Specks:**

1.  Hide all layers except Specks
2.  Go to Composition Menu…Add to Render Queue
3.  Select ‘High Quality or Lossless with Alpha’
4.  Click ‘Render
