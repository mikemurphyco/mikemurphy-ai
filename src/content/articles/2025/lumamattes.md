**How To Create Luma Mattes in Adobe Photoshop CC 2025:**

1.  Create New Document (1920 x 1080)
2.  Go to ‘Windows’ menu and click on ‘Timeline’
3.  Click ‘Create Video Timeline’
4.  Click on Hamburger Menu (3 Horizontal Lines) to the top right of Timeline
5.  Click on ‘Set Timeline Frame Rate’ to 24 fps
6.  Set Clip to 4 frames
7.  Duplicate Frame to have a total of 7 layers
8.  Set Color swatches to Black & White using keyboard shortcut D
9.  Choose a brush
10. Leave bottom layer all white
11. Go to next layer and brush on a little
12. Go to next layer above and brush on more
13. Repeat on all layers adding more brush strokes on each layer
14. The last layer should be all black

**How To Export Video from Photoshop:**

1.  Go to File Menu
2.  Click on ‘Export’
3.  Click on ‘Render Video’
4.  Enter Title
5.  Choose Destination
6.  Select ‘Adobe Media Encoder’ or ‘PNG Sequence’
7.  Select H.264
8.  1920 x 1080
9.  Click on Render

**How To Import Luma Matte from Photoshop to After Effects:**

1.  Open After Effects
2.  Drag Photoshop Luma Video to Project Panel of After Effects
3.  Create New Composition (1920 x 1080, 24fps, 10 secs)
4.  Drag Luma Matte Video on Timeline
5.  Right Click and select ‘Time…Enable Time Remapping’
6.  Adjust keyframes to change speed
7.  Add footage Layers

**How To Set Luma Matte in  After Effects:**

1.  Right-click on header and select Columns…Modes
2.  Select Footage Layer
3.  Click on pickwhip next to Track Mattes
4.  Drag to Luma Matte layer and release to hide visibility of Luma layer
5.  Tick the box to select ‘Luma Matte’
6.  Tick the box to Invert ‘Luma Matte’
7.  Tap Spacebar to Preview
