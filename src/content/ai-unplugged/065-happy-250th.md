## 🗞️ AI News of The Week: Fable 5 is Back!

Anthropic just restored Claude Fable 5 after a two-week export control suspension tied to a cybersecurity safeguard bypass.

On June 12, the US government applied export controls to Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5 after Amazon researchers found a way to prompt Fable 5 into identifying software vulnerabilities, and in one case, demonstrating how to exploit one.

Anthropic suspended access for all users while it worked with the government to close the gap. As of July 1, Fable 5 is back for everyone on [Claude.ai](https://claude.ai), the API, Claude Code, and Claude Cowork, backed by an improved safety classifier and a new industry-wide framework for scoring how dangerous a jailbreak actually is.

**Why this matters:**
- Anthropic's fix blocks the specific bypass technique in over 99% of cases
- Expect deeper government-Anthropic collaboration on pre-release testing for future frontier models

👉 [Read More About Redeploying Fable 5](https://www.anthropic.com/news/redeploying-fable-5)

## 🧠 AI Term of The Week: Second Brain

**Definition:**
An AI-powered knowledge base where the AI, not you, reads your raw notes and files and organizes them into a structured wiki.

**Explain It Like I'm 5:**
A "second brain" is like a magic treasure chest for your ideas. Instead of trying to remember every single thing in your head, you write down your favorite thoughts, stories, and pictures in a notebook or on a computer. When you need an idea, you just look inside.

**Why it matters now:**
- Popularized by Andrej Karpathy's "LLM Wiki" pattern: drop raw sources into a folder and an AI agent extracts the people, concepts, and ideas into linked wiki pages
- Flips the old "second brain" idea (manually organizing your own notes) into something the AI maintains for you
- Works well with tools like Obsidian, where your raw files and the AI-generated wiki live side by side
