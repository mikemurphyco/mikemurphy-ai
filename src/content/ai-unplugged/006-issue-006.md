### 🧠 **AI Term of The Week: LLM**

**LLM = Large Language Model** — is like a really smart computer program that has read a massive (huge) amount of text (like the entire internet). Because it has seen so many words and sentences, it has learned how to understand and generate text that sounds like it was written by a human.

**Examples of LLMs:**
ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, DeepSeek….all of ‘em.

 Imagine a student who has read almost every book, article, and website ever written. They would probably be pretty good at understanding what you're saying and writing thoughtful responses, right? An LLM is similar, but instead of reading, it analyzes text data to learn patterns and relationships between words. This allows it to do things like:

- **Answer your questions** in a comprehensive way.

- **Write different kinds of creative content**, like poems, code, scripts, musical pieces, email, letters, etc.

- **Translate languages.**

- **Summarize long pieces of text.**

- **Have conversations with you** in a chatbot.
