### 🧠 AI Term of The Week: Skill

**Definition:** A specialized bundle of instructions, knowledge, and capabilities that extends an AI agent's abilities to perform specific tasks or use particular tools.

**Explain Like I'm 5:** Think of skills like giving your AI assistant different job training courses. Just like teaching someone to be a chef (cooking skill) or electrician (wiring skill), you're teaching AI how to do specific tasks really well.

**Real examples:**

- **Not a skill:** Asking ChatGPT "Can you help me write a newsletter?"

- **Skill:** A pre-built "newsletter-draft" skill that knows your exact format, style guide, and template structure

- **Not a skill:** Telling Claude "Read this spreadsheet and analyze it"

- **Skill:** An "xlsx" skill with built-in knowledge of Excel functions, data cleaning, and chart generation

**Why it matters now:**

- Skills make AI agents dramatically more useful by teaching them specialized workflows instead of generic responses.

- You can create custom skills for your specific personal or business needs like for writing blog posts, podcast scripts, newsletters, etc.

- Skills are reusable and shareable, meaning you can build once and use across multiple projects.
