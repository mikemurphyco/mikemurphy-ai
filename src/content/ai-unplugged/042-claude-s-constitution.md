### 🧠 AI Term of The Week: Agentic

**Definition:** When AI systems can act autonomously to achieve goals, make decisions, and take actions without constant human intervention.

**Explain Like I’m 5:** The difference between a calculator (you control every step) and a personal assistant (you set the goal, they figure out how to achieve it).

**Real examples:**

- **Not agentic:** ChatGPT answering one question at a time as you ask

- **Agentic:** Claude Code automatically debugging your program, searching documentation, and implementing fixes across multiple files

- **Not agentic:** AI generating an image from your prompt

- **Agentic:** AI agent researching your brand, analyzing competitors, creating content strategy, and scheduling posts

**Why it matters now:**

- We're shifting from "AI tools" to "AI agents" in 2026

- Agentic systems can handle complex, multi-step workflows independently

- This is what enables AI to move from helpful assistant to actual productivity tools.
