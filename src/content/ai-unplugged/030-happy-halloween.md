**Summary:**
More than 850 prominent figures…AI pioneers, Nobel laureates, business leaders, cultural icons, and former military and political officials signed a statement coordinated by the Future of Life Institute, urging a halt to superintelligence development until clear safety and public consensus is achieved.

- **Core demands of the statement:**
The ban would prohibit developing AI systems that surpass human cognitive ability, unless there is scientific consensus on their safety, strong democratic oversight, and robust public support.

- **Risks and warnings:**
The letter draws on Alan Turing’s warning that superintelligent machines might inevitably take control once surpassing humans.

- **Industry context and high-profile absences:**
Major AI company leaders like Sam Altman (OpenAI) and Mustafa Suleyman (Microsoft AI) did not sign the statement, even as their companies press ahead.

### 🧠 **AI Term of The Week: WebHooks**

 WebHooks are often described as a "reverse API" because, instead of you making a request to an API, the API reaches out to you when something you want happens on the API.
Webhooks are used a lot in n8n automations and I always thought they were used when a service did not have an API. I was wrong.

- API’s → You call them

- WebHook → API’s call you

**Explain It Like I’m 5 Definition:**
You tell one computer: "Here is my address (a URL). If this one specific thing happens (like a payment goes through or a photo is uploaded), you must immediately send a message to this address."
