### 🧠 AI Term of The Week: Guardrails🚧

**Definition:** Safety constraints and rules built into AI systems to prevent harmful, unethical, or unintended outputs and actions.

**Explain Like I'm 5:** Guardrails for AI are like the bumpers at a bowling alley. They keep the ball (AI) from going into the gutter (dangerous territory) and help it stay on track toward the pins (helpful outcomes).

**Real examples:**

- **Not guardrails:** ChatGPT's training data and knowledge base

- **Guardrails:** ChatGPT refusing to help with illegal activities or harmful content

- **Not guardrails:** Claude's ability to write code and analyze images

- **Guardrails:** Claude's constitutional AI framework that shapes how it makes ethical decisions

**Why it matters now:**

- Open-source agents like 🦞 OpenClaw (formerly Moltbot) let users bypass standard safety controls, raising security concerns across the industry.

- As AI becomes more autonomous and agentic, guardrails shift from nice-to-have features to critical security infrastructure that prevents prompt injection attacks and unauthorized actions.
