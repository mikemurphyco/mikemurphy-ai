## 🗞️ AI News of The Week: Altman Says OpenAI Will Undercut Anthropic's AI Pricing by 75%

OpenAI is signaling an all-out price war with Anthropic, and it could change what you pay for AI tools built on top of these models.

Sam Altman said OpenAI's GPT-5.6 Sol is already priced at half of what Anthropic charges for Claude Fable 5, and that OpenAI would be "happy to deliver" its models at one-quarter of Anthropic's price, a 75% undercut. It's the clearest sign yet that the major labs are fighting for market share on cost, not just capability.

**Why this matters:**
- **Cheaper tokens for builders:** If you're paying per API call for AI features in your product, this pricing pressure could lower your costs fast
- **The race isn't just about smarts anymore:** Labs are competing on price the way cloud providers once did, which usually means faster, cheaper access for everyone
- **Anthropic is under pressure to respond:** Watch for pricing moves or new tiers from Anthropic in the coming weeks
- **A reminder that model access isn't a moat:** If the underlying AI is a commodity, your edge has to come from somewhere else (more on that below)

## 🧠 AI Term of The Week: Moat 🏰

**Definition:**
A moat is whatever makes it hard for competitors to copy or replace what you've built, the reason customers stick with you even when a cheaper option comes along.

**Explain It Like I'm 5:**
The metaphor goes back to medieval castles, where a wide moat kept invaders out. Warren Buffett is credited with bringing it into business language, writing that he looks for companies protected by "economic castles" with "unbreachable moats."

**Why it matters now:**
- Everyone building with AI is asking this question — When any startup can plug into the same models, "we use AI" stops being a moat
- Real moats in AI products tend to be data, distribution, workflow lock-in, or trust, not the model itself

**What is Remotion?**
Remotion is a framework that lets you build real MP4 videos out of React code, turning video creation into a coding and data problem instead of a timeline-editing one.

- Animations are fully programmable
- Scales rendering to the cloud for producing lots of videos fast
