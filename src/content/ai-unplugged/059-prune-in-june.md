### 🧠 AI Term of The Week: **GUI**

**Definition:**
Definition: GUI stands for Graphical User Interface. It's the visual layer of buttons, menus, windows, and icons you click on instead of typing commands.

 🔊` Sounds like “Gooey”`

**Real examples:**

- Opening Open WebUI (see below) in your browser and clicking a model from a dropdown

- Clicking "Restart" on a container inside the Hostinger dashboard

**Why it matters now:**

- Most powerful AI tools still live behind command lines (terminals), which scares off non-developers.

- A great GUI is the difference between an AI tool 100 people use and one a million people use.

[Open WebUI](https://openwebui.com/?utm_source=aiunplug.io&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=issue-59-prune-in-june)

**What is Open WebUI:**
A self-hosted, ChatGPT-style interface for running your own AI models privately, with full control over your data and zero monthly subscription.

**What it does:**

- Gives any local or API-based model (Ollama, OpenAI, Claude, Gemini) a clean chat interface in your browser

- Supports document upload, RAG, voice chat, image generation, and a built-in Python code interpreter

- Adds multi-user accounts, role-based access, and custom agents so a small team can share one private instance

- Looks and feels like ChatGPT, but nothing leaves your network

- Completely free and open source, no per-seat pricing as your team grows

**Real-world example:**
Open WebUI works great on a VPS with TailScale. Spin it up on a $7/month Hostinger box, lock it down behind your TailScale network, and you've got a private ChatGPT replacement on your phone, laptop, and iPad without exposing a single port to the public internet. (Tutorial coming soon).
