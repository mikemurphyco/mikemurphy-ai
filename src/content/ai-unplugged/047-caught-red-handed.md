### 🗞️ AI News of The Week: Caught Red-Handed 🇨🇳

**Anthropic Catches 3 Chinese Labs Stealing From Claude**
Anthropic published new research revealing they detected and stopped three separate Chinese AI labs from stealing Claude's capabilities through "distillation attacks."

**What are Distillation Attacks?**
Distillation attacks work by feeding a powerful model's outputs (like Claude's) into a competing model's training data, effectively copying its intelligence without building it from scratch. Anthropic's security team caught it happening in real time and published their findings.

**Why this matters:**

- It confirms what many suspected: competitors are using Claude's outputs to train their own models rather than doing the hard work themselves.

- Anthropic built detection systems sophisticated enough to catch this at scale, which is a major milestone in AI security.

- This raises serious questions about IP ownership in AI, and it's only going to get more complicated as models get smarter.

- For users and builders, it's a reminder that the trust you place in AI providers matters, and that some companies are cutting corners to keep up.

### 🧠 AI Term of The Week: Headless

**Definition:** Running software without a graphical user interface (GUI), so it operates in the background via commands or code instead of clicks and screens.

**Explain Like I'm 5:** Imagine driving a car with no steering wheel, dashboard, or windows. The engine still runs and the car still moves, but you control it through a remote instead of sitting inside. That's headless. The machine does its job, you just can't see or touch it directly.

**Real examples:**

- Not headless: Opening Chrome and manually clicking around a website.

- Headless: A script running Chrome invisibly in the background to automatically test your site or scrape data.

**Why it matters now:**

- More AI tools and automations run headless, meaning they work quietly in the background without a visual interface.

- Headless browsers power a lot of modern web scraping, testing, and AI agent tasks.

- Understanding headless systems helps you build smarter workflows that run 24/7 without you touching them.

**What is Cosmos?:**
Cosmos is an AI-powered mood board and visual research tool that lets creators collect, organize, and remix images, designs, and references to inspire and fuel generative AI projects.

**What it does:**

- Makes it easy to share mood boards with collaborators or use them as style references when prompting image generators like Midjourney or DALL-E.
