### 🧠 AI Term of The Week: **Telemetry**

**Definition:**
The automated collection and transmission of data from a system like an app or AI tool to a remote server for monitoring, analysis, and improvement.

**Explain It Like I’m 5:**
Telemetry is just **data sent from far away so you can see what’s happening**. For example, a car, rocket, or smartwatch can send information like speed, location, or temperature to another device or system It’s like a toy robot texting you its status so you know whether it’s okay or needs help.

**Why it matters now:**

- AI tools increasingly rely on telemetry to track usage patterns, catch errors, and improve models over time

- Understanding what telemetry does helps you make informed decisions about what data you're sharing with AI platforms

- Many self-hosted tools (n8n, Ollama, Open WebUI) let you disable telemetry entirely — useful if privacy is a priority on your VPS setup

### 🧰 AI Tool of The Week: Shlink

**What is LM Shlin:**
[Shlink](https://shlink.io/?utm_source=aiunplug.io&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=issue-62-fable-5-goes-public) is a free, open-source, self-hosted URL shortener with built-in analytics — giving you full control over your short links without relying on Bitly or Pretty Links.

**What it does:**

- Creates custom short URLs and QR codes hosted on your own domain

- Tracks every visit with detailed analytics: location, device, browser, and referrer

- Supports multiple domains, conditional redirects based on device or location, and per-link visit limits

**Mike’s real-world example:**
Run Shlink on your VPS to create branded short links like [go.yourdomain.com/newsletter](https://go.yourdomain.com/newsletter?utm_source=aiunplug.io&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=issue-62-fable-5-goes-public) — then see exactly how many people clicked, where they came from, and what device they used.
