### 🧠 **Word of the Week: Hallucinations**

 In the world of AI, a **hallucination** doesn’t mean trippy visuals — it means when an AI confidently makes something up.
**Hallucinations (in AI):** "When an AI, especially a generative AI like a chatbot, confidently presents false or nonsensical information as if it were true. It's like the AI is making things up, even though it sounds convincing."

 ChatGPT saying, “That plugin doesn’t exist”? That’s a hallucination.
Giving you a fake URL? Yep, hallucination.
Inventing a historical fact? You guessed it.

**AI hallucinations are a reminder:**
AI is smart, but it’s not grounded in truth. It’s grounded in **patterns**.
So, if you’re using AI to create — whether it’s code, copy, or a sticker pack — keep your human filter on as AI was a Deadhead in a former lifetime.
